Starbucks Store Performance

"Pune store sales dipped last quarter — why, and what do we do about it?" A VP-of-Operations question, answered end-to-end.

🔧 Approach

  • Diagnosed 45,440 transactions across 5 Pune stores (Q2 2025 vs Q2 2026) — first in SQL, then visualised in Power BI.
  • Split the top line into revenue vs units vs visits using conditional aggregation (SUM(CASE WHEN year…)) to see what was really moving.
  • Cross-checked the story with a second independent metric so the conclusion couldn't be a fluke.

📊 What I found

  • Revenue fell only −5.1% — but units −12.2% and visits −11.8%. An +8% price hike masked a ~12% footfall crash.
  • It wasn't city-wide — just 2 of 5 stores: Hinjewadi IT Park −51% (WFH emptied it) and FC Road −13% (new competitor). The other 3 grew +11–14%.
  • Revenue and visits told the same store-by-store story → a robust, defensible conclusion.

✅ Insights for change

  • Hinjewadi — push delivery / app pickup to reach the work-from-home crowd.
  • FC Road — loyalty combos to win footfall back from the competitor.
  • Pause further price hikes at the struggling stores until visits recover.
The difference this makes: "sales are down 5%" becomes a store-specific, cause-specific action plan — the average hid two failing stores behind three healthy ones.

Dataset is synthetic (generated for this case study) — not real Starbucks data.
