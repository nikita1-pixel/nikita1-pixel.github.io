About

From behind the counter to behind the data.

I was a Starbucks barista who couldn't stop thinking in patterns — how many lattes before the morning rush, which days ran out of oat milk first, when to prep for the 3 PM slump. I didn't know it yet, but I was already doing data analysis; I just didn't have the tools. Now I do — and the chaos I learned to read behind the counter is the same chaos I find inside every messy dataset.

I'm a BCA graduate (2026, MIT-WPU Pune) and a self-driven Data Analyst. I've cleaned 1.3M real loan records, shipped a live web app, and built executive dashboards in Power BI and Tableau. Here's how I work:

01Clean first, ask questions always. Bad data means bad decisions — I figure out what's missing, broken or misleading before I build anything.
02Dashboards that get used, not just admired. I design for the person reading it. If they can't make a decision in 10 seconds, I redesign it.
03Honest analysis, not confirmation bias. I follow where the data leads — even when the answer isn't the one someone hoped for.
